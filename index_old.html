Research

My current research lies at the intersection of synthetic biology, microfluidics, and antimicrobial resistance (AMR). I develop mother machine microfluidic platforms to study bacterial behaviour at the single-cell level, combining experimental and computational approaches to better understand microbial dynamics and enable the design of engineered biological systems. My interest in synthetic biology began during my bachelor's degree. At my previous position at Inito, I worked on developing home testing kits for whole blood assay for various hormones. This involved identifying the problems existing in whole blood assays and also attempting to solve them. I used synthetic biology approaches like protein engineering to develop synthetic antibodies for the tests that would give better performance and results.

Over the past few years I have built my skills in mutational library preparation, microscopy, automation, protein engineering, protein expression and purification, structure prediction and docking, designing biomolecular systems, CRN modeling and simulation and various molecular biology techniques. The details of my completed research work are mentioned on the research page. With my interest in synthetic biology, I aim to make biomolecular systems that would potentially be used in real-world applications such as healthcare and sustainability.
